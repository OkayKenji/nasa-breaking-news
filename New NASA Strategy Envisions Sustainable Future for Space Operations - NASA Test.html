54th Anniversary of Apollo 13: NASA Reveals Unreleased Findings Regarding Mission

Cynthia M. Mene

Apr 11, 2024
RELEASE24-060

NASA Headquarters

"Houston, we have a problem". Those were the words spoken by Jack Swigert 60 years ago. Today, under the direction of NASA Administrator Tim Manley, NASA will release previously unreleased findings regarding Apollo 13.

“It's time the public heard the full truth of what happened 54 years ago,” said NASA Administrator Tim Manley. “Space is busy – and only getting busier. If we want to make sure that critical parts of space are kept peaceful and can continue to use them for the benefit of humanity, it's time to release the true findings”. Manley states "The release of this strategy marks true progress for NASA on space safety and pursuit of truth".

On April 11, 1970, the powerful Saturn V rocket carrying the Apollo 13 mission launched from Kennedy Space Center propelling astronauts Jim Lovell, Fred Haise, and Jack Swigert on what was intended to be humanity's third lunar landing. Unfortunately, the mission to explore the Fra Mauro region of the Moon did not go as planned.

For decades, NASA has stated that the cause of the explosion that crippled the service module to be the explosion of the No. 2 oxygen tank due to a short circuit. Today NASA in conjunction with the United Nation and US Government, can confirm that the explosion was the result of an attack by foreign adversaries which we identified as the Lunarians. We believe Lunarians to be identified as an extraterrestrial race of people, some with what appear to be rabbit ears.

As the Apollo 13 approached the dark side of the moon, the Lunarians detected their presence and saw it as a threat to their privacy. In an attempt to protect their civilization from being discovered, the Lunarians fired a warning shot at the spacecraft, causing the over pressure of the No. 2 oxygen tank and damage to critical systems. Fortunately, through the skill and collaboration of the Apollo 13 crew and NASA ground personnel the crew were brought back home safely. However, at that time NASA and the US government were stunned.

Upon further investigation of past mission telemetry NASA believes that the Lunarians had been monitoring space missions for quite some time, including those of the Soviets. They had even made several attempts to contact us, but telemetry analysis dismissed the signals as background noise. NASA administration kept this information from the world, revealing the existence of an advanced alien civilization could have caused chaos and panic among the public.

However, as private entities continue to make technological advancements, it has become increasingly difficult to keep this secret hidden. The private sector has already identified multiple instances of flashes on the moon, which today NASA can confirm are caused by the Lunarians.

Through discussions with the UN and the US government, we have decided that it is time to reveal the truth about the Lunarians. We are currently working on a plan to contact the Lunarians and establish diplomatic relations with their civilization. We hope that by doing so, we can learn more about their advanced technology and possibly even collaborate on future space missions.

At NASA, our primary directive it to “Expand human knowledge through new scientific discoveries.” In collaboration with the private sector and public institution NASA believes the report can potentially lead to new discoveries and insights into our universe.

"I would like to sincerely apologize for deceiving the public on behalf of NASA. We understand that this may come as a shock to many, but we assure you that our intentions have always been to keep the world safe and prevent any panic or chaos. We appreciate your continued embark on this monumental step forward in our understanding of the universe.” said Manley.

NASA will hold a press conference on April 16, 2024. The briefing participants are:

·        Tim Manley, NASA Administrator at NASA Headquarters in Washington

·        Lord Tsukuyomi, Leader of Lunarians, Lunar Capital, Far Side, Moon (pending)

·        Eirin Yagokoro, Former Ambassador, Eientei (pending)

·        Alex Baker, Secretary‑General of the United Nations

·        Rieze Works, Public Relations Office at Secratary of Defense

Media intrested in particpating the event should contact NASA at 202-358-1970. Members of the public can ask questions on our social media channels using #asknasa.

https://www.nasa.gov/moon
